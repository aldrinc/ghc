## Platform Specifications Table

### Meta Primary Text

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Character limit** | First 125 characters visible before "See more" truncation. Full text up to 2,200 characters. |
| **Hook constraint** | Complete hook (all 3 anatomy components) must land within first 125 characters. If impossible, pattern interrupt + relevance signal within 125; implicit promise is first line after fold. |
| **Adaptation rules** | Front-load the interrupt. No slow buildup. No "In today's world" or throat-clearing openers (Section 3 anti-pattern #1). Write as if only the first 125 characters will be read. |
| **Compliance** | No disease claims in ad copy. No personal-attribute targeting ("Are you suffering from...?"). Health disclaimers required per Section 4 Subsection E. Landing page must match ad claims. |
| **Brand note** | Best archetypes: Problem Callout, Curiosity Gap, Safety Warning. Avoid Story/Anecdote (needs too much space). Tone: calm, specific, protective. |

### Meta Video/Reel

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Time limit** | First 1-3 seconds determine retention. |
| **Hook constraint** | Text overlay carries pattern interrupt. Spoken word delivers relevance signal. Visual establishes context or emotional tone. All three must land within 3 seconds. |
| **Adaptation rules** | Text overlay: standalone interrupt, 8-12 words max. Spoken script: opens mid-thought, no greetings or preamble. Visual: must not require explanation. |
| **Compliance** | Same claim restrictions as primary text. Branded content toggle required for partnerships. On-screen disclaimers for health-related claims. |
| **Brand note** | Best archetypes: Identity Callout, Contrarian Claim. Text overlay example: "The herb in your cabinet that interacts with blood thinners." Spoken word opens with the relevance signal immediately. |

### TikTok

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Time limit** | 1-2 seconds to hook before the swipe. |
| **Character limit** | 150-character text overlay limit for readability. |
| **Hook constraint** | Open mid-thought. No introductions, no "Hey guys," no brand name in first 2 seconds. First spoken sentence is the pattern interrupt. Text overlay reinforces or adds curiosity gap. |
| **Adaptation rules** | Write as if the viewer stumbled into the middle of a conversation. Use contrarian or curiosity archetypes. Specificity outperforms vague claims. Must pass the "real person, not advertisement" test. |
| **Compliance** | No health condition claims. No claims discouraging professional consultation. AI monitoring is active -- avoid near-violations. Branded content toggle required for paid partnerships. |
| **Brand note** | Best archetypes: Curiosity Gap, Contrarian Claim, Identity Callout, Vulnerable Confession. Example opening: "So I just found out the herb I have been giving my kid interacts with his prescription." Conversational, native-feeling, specific. |

### YouTube Pre-Roll

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Time limit** | 5-second skip window. Hook must hold through the skip decision. |
| **Hook constraint** | Most compelling claim or most specific detail must appear in first sentence (under 5 seconds spoken). Do not open with dismissable questions. Do not open with "What if I told you..." |
| **Adaptation rules** | First sentence = single most interesting, specific thing. Second sentence = relevance signal that earns 5 more seconds. Promise must land by second 10. Front-load value; earn attention in increments. |
| **Compliance** | No false or exaggerated health claims in ad or on landing page. No focus on specific body parts or health conditions. Landing page reviewed alongside ad content. Google enforces account-level consequences without warning. |
| **Brand note** | Best archetypes: Safety Warning, Expert Insight, Direct Benefit. Lead with the most specific, unexpected fact. Example: "Most herb-drug interaction charts are missing three factors that determine whether an herb is actually safe for you." |

### YouTube Organic

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Time limit** | First 15-30 seconds set retention. Longer hook runway than pre-roll. |
| **Hook constraint** | Pattern interrupt in first sentence. Relevance signal by second sentence. Promise can develop through second 15. More room for story or expert-insight archetypes. |
| **Adaptation rules** | Match hook to search intent. If video targets "how to use herbs safely," hook must reference safety within 10 seconds. Specificity and credentials outperform hype. |
| **Compliance** | Same as pre-roll. Landing page and video content both evaluated. Testimonials in video require disclaimers. |
| **Brand note** | Best archetypes: Expert Insight, Contrarian Claim, Story/Anecdote. This platform allows the most nuanced hooks. Example: "After reviewing over 300 herbs, I found three safety factors that almost no herbal guide covers. Here is what they are and why they matter." |

### Email Subject Line + Preview Text

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Character limit** | Subject line: 40-60 characters for display. Preview text: approximately 90 additional characters. |
| **Hook constraint** | Subject line carries pattern interrupt. Preview text carries relevance signal or implicit promise. They function as a pair, not independently. |
| **Adaptation rules** | Subject line: lowercase-friendly, conversational tone, 4-9 words. No ALL CAPS. Preview text: extends the subject line's thought, does not repeat it. The pair must create enough pull to earn the open without overpromising. |
| **Compliance** | Subject line must accurately reflect email content (CAN-SPAM). No deceptive subject lines. No disease claims. Health disclaimers required in email body. |
| **Brand note** | Best archetypes: Curiosity Gap, Safety Warning, Identity Callout. Example pair -- Subject: "the herb interaction most people miss" / Preview: "it is probably in your kitchen right now -- here is what to check." |

### Presell Headline

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Character limit** | No character limit. |
| **Hook constraint** | Must feel like natural continuation of the ad hook. If ad promised a specific insight, headline must reference that insight. Mismatch causes immediate bounce. |
| **Adaptation rules** | Write presell headline AFTER the ad hook, not before. The presell headline answers or extends the ad's implicit promise. Use editorial, not sales, framing. Should read like a magazine feature. |
| **Compliance** | All Section 4 claim restrictions apply. Landing page evaluated alongside ad content by all platforms. Claims on presell cannot exceed claims in ad. |
| **Calibration by type** | Listicle: 8-14 words, number + curiosity/specificity. Advertorial: 10-18 words, news/discovery angle, passes "would a magazine print this?" test. See WORKFLOW.md Section 4 for full specs. |
| **Brand note** | Message match is the single most important factor. A well-matched but mediocre headline outperforms a brilliant but mismatched one. |

### Sales Page Headline

| Parameter | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Character limit** | No character limit (but see word count ranges in WORKFLOW.md Section 4). |
| **Hook constraint** | Must confirm relevance and create pull to scroll. If reader arrives from presell, reference what they just learned. If from ad, match ad promise. If from direct traffic, lead with core differentiator. |
| **Adaptation rules** | Specificity outperforms cleverness. State core value proposition in concrete terms. One clear idea per headline. Can use 3-part stack (pre-head + headline + sub-head) per WORKFLOW.md Section 5. |
| **Compliance** | All Section 4 restrictions. No banned phrases. Headline sets claim ceiling for entire page. |
| **Brand note** | Best archetypes: Direct Benefit, Social Proof Lead, Expert Insight, Safety Warning. The sales page headline is the most important single headline in the conversion path. It must be simultaneously specific, credible, and compelling. |

## Adaptation Process (Step-by-Step)

The agent follows this exact sequence when adapting any headline concept across platforms.

**Step 1: Write at full length.**
Write the headline with all three anatomy components (pattern interrupt, relevance signal, implicit promise) clearly articulated, without platform constraints. This is the "master" version.

**Step 2: Identify the target platform.**
Determine from the brief which platform the headline will be deployed on. If multiple platforms, create separate adapted versions for each.

**Step 3: Apply character/time constraint.**
Fit the headline within the platform's limits. If it does not fit, apply the priority hierarchy:
1. Pattern interrupt (always keep)
2. Relevance signal (keep if possible)
3. Implicit promise (move after fold if necessary)

**Step 4: Check compliance.**
Cross-reference the adapted headline against the platform's compliance notes above and Section 4 constraints. Flag any violations.

**Step 5: Verify anatomy.**
Confirm the adapted headline still passes the three-component anatomy check. If a component was cut for space, verify it appears:
- Immediately after the fold (Meta primary text)
- In the preview text (email)
- In the first seconds of body copy/script (video)
- In the sub-head (sales page)

**Step 6: Verify page-type calibration.**
Confirm the adapted headline meets the word count, tone, and structure requirements for its page type per WORKFLOW.md Section 4.

**Step 7: Run self-evaluation.**
Execute the full self-evaluation checklist from WORKFLOW.md Section 12 on the adapted version.

## Platform Priority for Headline Development

When developing headlines for a multi-platform campaign, write them in this order:

1. **Ad headline** (Meta/TikTok/YouTube) -- this is the origin point of the conversion path.
2. **Presell headline** -- must message-match the ad headline.
3. **Sales page headline** -- must message-match the presell headline.
4. **Email subject line** -- can be adapted from any of the above or written independently for list segments.

This sequence ensures message match is built into the development process rather than checked after the fact.

---

## Cross-Platform Consistency Rules

1. The core promise must remain consistent across all platforms, even as the expression changes.
2. If the ad uses a specific number ("7 herbs"), that number must appear or be referenced on the presell and sales page.
3. If the ad names a specific enemy ("contradictory information"), that enemy must be acknowledged on downstream pages.
4. Tone may shift slightly across platforms (TikTok is more conversational, sales page is more direct) but must remain within the brand voice boundaries defined in Section 3.
5. No platform adaptation may introduce a claim that is not present in the master headline or the upstream ad. Claims can only be maintained or narrowed, never expanded, as you move down the funnel.
