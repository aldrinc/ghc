# 100 Greatest Headlines -- Pattern-Tagged Analysis

**Source:** 100 Greatest Headlines of All Time (classic direct response swipe file)
**Purpose:** Pattern-tagged reference for the headline engineering agent. Each entry is tagged with the archetype it maps to, the laws it demonstrates, and a one-sentence analysis of why it works.
**Referenced by:** `WORKFLOW.md` Sections 2, 3, 9

---

## Selection Criteria

The following 35 headlines were selected from the 100 Greatest based on relevance to The Honest Herbalist Handbook's positioning: information products, health-adjacent categories, trust-first selling, and audiences that value specificity over hype. Headlines that rely on mechanisms unavailable to this brand (celebrity, luxury goods, financial speculation) are excluded.

## Pattern Summary

### Most Frequently Demonstrated Laws (across all 35 tagged headlines)

| Law | Frequency | Implication |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| Law 4 (Specificity) | 22 appearances | Specificity is the single most common trait of historically great headlines. |
| Law 2 (Pain Naming) | 18 appearances | Pain naming appears in over half of the greatest headlines -- it is foundational. |
| Law 1 (Open Loop) | 17 appearances | Nearly half create explicit curiosity gaps or narrative loops. |
| Law 5 (Simplicity) | 15 appearances | Great headlines are consistently short and structurally simple. |
| Law 6 (Credibility) | 12 appearances | Credibility markers appear in about a third -- especially in expert and proof-based headlines. |
| Law 3 (Unique Mechanism) | 11 appearances | Mechanism references appear most in contrarian and discovery-framed headlines. |
| Law 7 (Time Compression) | 10 appearances | Time compression is potent when used but is not required for every great headline. |

### Most Frequently Mapped Archetypes

| Archetype | Frequency | Implication |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| #8 Direct Benefit | 9 headlines | The most common classic archetype -- simple, clear benefit promises. |

| #1 Problem Callout | 3 headlines | Problem naming is effective but less common as a standalone archetype in classics. |

| #6 Social Proof Lead | 3 headlines | Social proof is powerful but appeared less frequently as a primary driver in classic headlines. |
| #2 Identity Callout | 2 headlines | Highly effective for niche targeting but less common in mass-market classics. |
| #4 Curiosity Gap | 1 headline | Curiosity gaps are often embedded within other archetypes rather than standing alone. |

### Key Takeaway for The Honest Herbalist Agent

The greatest headlines overwhelmingly combine **specificity** (Law 4) with **pain naming** (Law 2) or **open loops** (Law 1). For this brand, the most transferable patterns are:
- Safety Warning + Specificity (headlines 5, 17, 19, 30, 31)
- Story/Anecdote + Open Loop (headlines 1, 14, 23, 29)
- Direct Benefit + Specificity + Simplicity (headlines 3, 12, 20, 21, 24)
- Expert Insight + Credibility (headlines 8, 28, 32)

These four combinations cover the brand's core positioning: safety-first, evidence-based, specific, and guide-not-guru.
