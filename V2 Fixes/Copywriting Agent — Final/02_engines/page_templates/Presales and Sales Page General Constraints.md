# **Direct Response Copy Spec Sheet**

## **Presell Listicle · Presell Advertorial · Sales Page**

Use this document as binding constraints when generating copy. Every spec below is grounded in direct response (DR) principles first, then validated by attention-science research on digital reading behavior.

---

## **Governing DR Principles (Apply to ALL Three Formats)**

1. **One Reader, One Offer, One Action.** Every piece exists to move one person toward one CTA. Eliminate anything that doesn't serve the next click.  
2. **Copy length is dictated by the amount of selling the piece must do.** A presell listicle does less selling than a sales page — so it should be proportionally shorter. Never pad.  
3. **Every sentence must earn the next sentence.** (Eugene Schwartz) If a line doesn't create curiosity, build belief, or advance the argument, cut it.  
4. **Specificity \> generality.** Concrete numbers, names, mechanisms, and timelines outperform vague claims in every split test.  
5. **Proof density scales with ask size.** Listicles need light proof. Advertorials need moderate proof. Sales pages need heavy, stacked proof.  
6. **The lead does 80% of the work.** First 100-200 words determine whether anything else gets read.  
7. **Pattern interrupt at the top, open loop throughout.** Readers must be stopped (interrupt) then pulled forward (loop).  
8. **Temperature-aware copy.** Presell pieces warm traffic; the sales page closes it. Don't close in a presell. Don't re-warm on a sales page.

## **FORMAT 1: PRESELL LISTICLE**

### **Purpose**

Bridge between ad and sales page/advertorial. Build curiosity, lower skepticism, and frame the offer before the reader arrives at the selling environment.

### **DR Framework**

* **Structure:** Fascination-stack format. Each list item is a mini open loop.  
* **Persuasion mode:** Curiosity \+ social proof \+ novelty. NOT feature/benefit selling.  
* **CTA style:** Soft editorial transition ("See how it works →"), NOT hard close.

### **Length & Dimensions**

| Element | Spec |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Total word count** | **800 – 1,200 words** (never exceed 1,400) |
| **Headline** | 8 – 14 words. Number \+ curiosity/specificity. |
| **Intro/deck** | 80 – 120 words. Establish context, pattern interrupt, open the first loop. |
| **Number of list items** | 5 – 7 (5 is default; 7 max) |
| **Words per list item** | 100 – 160 words each |
| **Subheads (item titles)** | 6 – 12 words. Each must be a fascination that could stand alone. |
| **CTA / outro** | 40 – 80 words. One link/button. Editorial tone. |
| **Paragraphs** | 1 – 3 sentences max per paragraph |
| **Reading time target** | 3 – 5 minutes |

### **Attention Science Constraints**

* Average content page gets \~37 seconds of shallow attention; listicles extend this via chunked scanning. Subheads must be independently compelling because \~60% of readers scan only those.  
* Front-load the strongest 2 items in positions 1 and 2 (primacy effect) and place a strong item last (recency effect). Middle items are read least.  
* No item should require scrolling more than one mobile screen length (\~100-130 words).

### **What NOT to Do**

* Don't exceed 7 items (diminishing returns \+ fatigue).  
* Don't turn list items into mini-essays. Each item \= one idea, one proof point, one loop.  
* Don't reveal the full mechanism. The listicle teases; the next page explains.  
* Don't include price, bonuses, or guarantee language. That belongs on the sales page.

## **FORMAT 2: PRESELL ADVERTORIAL**

### **Purpose**

Educate, build belief in the mechanism, and position the offer as the logical solution — all inside an editorial wrapper that reads like discovered content, not advertising.

### **DR Framework**

* **Structure:** Problem → Discovery Story → Mechanism Reveal → Proof → Soft CTA  
* **Persuasion mode:** Story-driven belief building. The "mechanism sale" happens here.  
* **CTA style:** 2 CTAs — one mid-article (after mechanism reveal), one at close. Editorial language.  
* **Voice:** Third-person journalistic OR first-person testimonial. Must read as content, not pitch.

### **Length & Dimensions**

| Element | Spec |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Total word count** | **1,200 – 1,800 words** (never exceed 2,000) |
| **Headline** | 10 – 18 words. News/discovery angle. Must pass the "would a magazine print this?" test. |
| **Byline/source line** | Include if format supports it. Adds editorial credibility. |
| **Lead (opening section)** | 150 – 250 words. Hook via story, startling fact, or contrarian claim. |
| **Problem/agitation section** | 200 – 350 words. Make the reader feel the problem viscerally. |
| **Mechanism/discovery section** | 300 – 450 words. This is the core belief-building section. Explain WHY the solution works using a named mechanism or unique framework. |
| **Proof/evidence section** | 200 – 350 words. Stats, studies, testimonials, authority citations. Match proof type to audience sophistication. |
| **CTA / transition** | 60 – 100 words per CTA instance. Link to sales page. |
| **Paragraphs** | 1 – 3 sentences. Occasional single-sentence paragraphs for pacing. |
| **Reading time target** | 5 – 7 minutes |

### **Attention Science Constraints**

* Long-form editorial content follows an "F-pattern" scan: readers engage deeply with the first 2-3 paragraphs, then increasingly scan. Front-load the most compelling proof and story beats early.  
* Engagement drops sharply after \~1,800 words for advertorial-style content. The 2,000-word hard cap protects against this.  
* Include 2-3 subheads that function as re-entry points for scanners. These should be benefit- or curiosity-driven, not generic ("The Science Behind It" → bad; "Why Cardiologists Are Calling This 'The 30-Second Reset'" → good).  
* Pull quotes or bold callouts every 300-400 words re-engage drifting attention.

### **What NOT to Do**

* Don't pitch the product by name until the mechanism is established (typically not before word 600).  
* Don't include pricing, bonus stacks, or order forms. Advertorial sells the click, not the product.  
* Don't use hype language ("amazing," "incredible," "revolutionary") — it breaks the editorial frame.  
* Don't stack more than 3 testimonials. One strong story \> five weak quotes.  
* Don't write in an overtly promotional voice. If it reads like a sales page, it fails as an advertorial.

## **FORMAT 3: SALES PAGE (Warm Traffic from Presell)**

### **Purpose**

Convert pre-sold traffic into buyers. Handle remaining objections, present the full offer, establish value, reverse risk, and close.

### **DR Framework**

* **Structure:** Headline → Lead → Problem Restatement → Solution/Mechanism (brief, since presell covered it) → Offer Stack → Proof → Risk Reversal → Urgency → CTA → P.S.  
* **Persuasion mode:** Offer architecture \+ objection handling \+ emotional close.  
* **CTA style:** Direct, clear, repeated. Minimum 3 CTA buttons distributed through the page.  
* **Key DR principle:** The presell already built belief in the mechanism. The sales page's job is to build belief in the OFFER (value, trust, risk).

### **Length & Dimensions**

| Element | Spec |
| ----- | ----- |
| **Total word count** | **2,000 – 3,500 words** for offers ≤$197 with presold traffic. Add 500–1,000 words for $197–$497. For $497+ consider dedicated VSL \+ page. |
| **Headline** | 8 – 20 words. Biggest promise or transformation. Can use a pre-head (5-8 words) \+ headline \+ sub-head (15-25 words) stack. |
| **Lead section** | 150 – 300 words. Re-engage from the presell, restate the core promise, create "yes momentum." |
| **Problem restatement** | 100 – 200 words. Brief — the reader already feels this from the presell. Don't re-agitate at length. |
| **Solution / mechanism recap** | 150 – 300 words. Bridge from presell. Summarize, don't re-teach. |
| **Offer stack** | 200 – 400 words. Core offer \+ 2-4 bonuses, each with a name, value anchor, and one-line benefit. |
| **Proof section** | 300 – 500 words. Testimonials, results, credentials, data. Stack 3-5 proof elements. |
| **Risk reversal (guarantee)** | 80 – 150 words. Name the guarantee. Be specific about terms. |
| **Urgency / scarcity** | 50 – 100 words. Only if genuine. Fake urgency erodes trust on presold traffic. |
| **Closing CTA** | 50 – 100 words \+ button. Restate transformation \+ remove final objection. |
| **P.S. section** | 1 – 2 P.S. items, 40 – 80 words each. Restate strongest benefit and/or guarantee. |
| **Paragraphs** | 1 – 4 sentences. Short paragraphs dominate; occasional longer paragraph for story beats. |
| **Reading time target** | 8 – 14 minutes |

### **Attention Science Constraints**

* Sales pages are scanned in a "layer cake" pattern: readers jump between headline, subheads, bold text, buttons, price, and guarantee — then decide whether to read body copy. Every scannable element must independently convey value.  
* Decision fatigue increases after \~3,000 words. For presold traffic, this is the ceiling for standard offers. Beyond this, each additional section must clear a higher bar.  
* Place the first CTA button no later than 40% of the way through the page. Most conversions on warm-traffic pages happen at the first or second CTA, not the bottom.  
* Guarantee section is the second-most-read section after the headline. Never bury it.  
* Testimonials with specifics (numbers, timeframes, names) are processed as evidence; vague praise is processed as filler and skipped.

### **What NOT to Do**

* Don't re-teach the mechanism at length. The presell did that. A brief recap is enough.  
* Don't use more than 4 bonuses. Bonus fatigue dilutes perceived value after the 4th item.  
* Don't bury the price. Price anchoring → investment reveal should happen clearly, not hidden in running text.  
* Don't write 5,000+ words for a sub-$100 offer on warm traffic. The length signals "this needs a lot of convincing" — which contradicts the presell's work.  
* Don't open with a long origin story. The reader is presold; get to the offer faster than you would on a cold-traffic page.  
* Don't repeat the same proof point in different words. Stack diverse proof types (testimonial, data, authority, demonstration, social proof).

## **Quick Reference: Length Guardrails**

| Format | Floor | Target | Hard Ceiling |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Presell Listicle | 800 words | 1,000 words | 1,400 words |
| Presell Advertorial | 1,200 words | 1,500 words | 2,000 words |
| Sales Page (≤$197 warm) | 2,000 words | 2,800 words | 3,500 words |
| Sales Page ($197-$497 warm) | 2,500 words | 3,500 words | 4,500 words |

## **CTA Density Rule**

* **Listicle:** 1 CTA (end only)  
* **Advertorial:** 2 CTAs (mid-article \+ end)  
* **Sales Page:** 3 – 4 CTAs (after mechanism, after offer stack, after guarantee, P.S.)

## **Global Formatting Rules (All Formats)**

* Maximum paragraph length: 4 sentences  
* Default paragraph length: 1 – 2 sentences  
* Subhead frequency: every 200 – 400 words  
* Bold text: use for key claims, transitions, and benefit statements only — not for emphasis of random words  
* ALL-CAPS: headline and subheads only, never in body copy  
* No walls of text longer than 5 lines on mobile (\~80 words without a visual break)
