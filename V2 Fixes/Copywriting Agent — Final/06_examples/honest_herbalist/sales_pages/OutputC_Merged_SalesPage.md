## What Makes This Handbook Structurally Different

Most herb guides are organized around what an herb does. This one is organized around what you need to know before you use it.

Every herb profile starts with three things most references skip:

**1. Dosing ranges — by form and by age group.**
Not "brew a tea." Actual milligram ranges for tinctures, capsules, teas, and glycerites. Adult ranges and pediatric ranges, clearly separated. When a dose is based on published data, it says so. When it's traditional use, it says that too.

**2. Interaction flags for common medications.**
SSRIs. Blood thinners. Birth control. Blood pressure meds. If an herb has a documented interaction, that flag is on the profile — not buried in an appendix.

**3. A confidence rating for every listed use.**
Three-tier system — *Traditional Use*, *Some Evidence*, *Stronger Evidence* — so you know the difference between folklore and a controlled study.
