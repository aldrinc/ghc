## What Makes This Handbook Structurally Different

Most herb guides are organized around what an herb does. This one is organized around what you need to know before you use it.

Every herb profile in The Honest Herbalist Handbook starts with three things most references skip:

**1. Dosing ranges — by form and by age group.**
Not "a few drops" or "brew a tea." Actual milligram ranges for tinctures, capsules, teas, and glycerites. Adult ranges and pediatric ranges, clearly separated. When a dose is based on published clinical data, it says so. When it's based on traditional use, it says that too.

**2. Interaction flags for common medications.**
SSRIs. Blood thinners. Birth control. Blood pressure meds. If an herb has a documented interaction with a medication that millions of people take daily, that flag is on the profile — not buried in an appendix you'll never read.

**3. A confidence rating for every listed use.**
Not every herb has the same weight of evidence. This handbook uses a three-tier system — *Traditional Use*, *Some Evidence*, *Stronger Evidence* — so you know the difference between "people have used this for centuries" and "this was tested in a controlled study."
