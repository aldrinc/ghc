# Section 2: Page-Type Templates with Annotated Structural Logic
## Section-by-Section Blueprints for Every Funnel Asset

**Brand context:** The Honest Herbalist Handbook | $49 digital herbal reference | DTC health/wellness
**Audience:** Women 25-55, safety-conscious, anti-hype, "crunchy but not anti-science," Stage 5 market sophistication
**Companion docs:** Subsection A covers awareness mapping, belief sequencing, agitation calibration, and presell-vs-sales-page structural differences. This document EXTENDS that framework with operational blueprints, CTA rules, failure modes, and the cross-page flow map.

---

## CROSS-PAGE FLOW MAP

```
Meta/TikTok Ad --> PRESELL (B1-B4) --> SALES PAGE (B5-B8, B1-B4 recap)
    --> CHECKOUT --> THANK YOU --> UPSELL --> [if no] DOWNSELL --> EMAIL SEQUENCES
```

### Belief State at Each Transition

| Transition | Required State | If Not Met |
|---|---|---|
| Ad --> Presell | Curiosity triggered. No belief prerequisites. | Presell headline must hook in 3 seconds or bounce. |
| Presell --> Sales Page | B1-B4 held: herbs help but aren't magic; natural isn't safe; ecosystem is broken; she needs a system. | Sales Page Section 2 (Problem Recap) compresses B1-B4 into 150-200 words for direct-traffic readers. |
| Sales Page --> Checkout | B5-B8 held: handbook is different; natural-first + responsible; $49 justified; risk is low. | Checkout reinforces B7-B8 (value + guarantee) or she abandons. |
| Checkout --> Thank You | Purchase complete. Satisfaction + mild anxiety. | First line must confirm order. Delay = refund risk. |
| Thank You --> Upsell | Post-purchase high. Open but price-sensitive. | Upsell frames enhancement, never incompleteness. |
| Upsell --> Downsell | Said no. Friction fatigue. Patience thin. | Downsell must be shorter, simpler, more generous. |

### Non-Standard Entry Handling

| Entry Point | Handling |
|---|---|
| **Direct to Sales Page** | Problem Recap section expands. Treats reader as Problem Aware. B1-B4 compressed but present. |
| **Direct to Checkout** (cart recovery) | Mini value restatement + guarantee. No belief-building. |
| **Email to Sales Page** | Reader is Solution Aware. Lead with mechanism (B5), not problem (B3). |
| **Upsell without purchase** | Redirect to sales page. Upsell copy requires purchase context. |

## GLOBAL RULES (Apply to All Page Types)

### Mobile Rules (This Audience Is 70%+ Mobile)

- Mobile is primary. Desktop is the adaptation. Any layout that breaks on 375px is rejected.
- No paragraph longer than 4 lines on mobile (~40-50 words).
- All CTA buttons: full-width on mobile, minimum 44px tap target, thumb-reachable.
- No horizontal scrolling, no tables in rendered copy, no side-by-side layouts.
- Pages must load in under 3 seconds on 4G. No autoplay video. Images lazy-loaded.
- Links in emails: own line, large enough to tap. No multi-column email layouts. Max width 600px.

### CTA Consistency Rules

- CTA language must be consistent within a page and across the funnel for the same action.
- Language prohibition (all pages): no "Buy Now," "Add to Cart," "Purchase," "Submit," or "Pay Now."
- "Get" is ownership-framed. "Start using" is action-framed. "Complete My Order" is confirmation-framed. All acceptable.
- Every CTA button must state or imply what happens next. Ambiguity kills clicks.

### Enforcement Checklist (Before Any Draft Is Complete)

1. Declare: what beliefs does the reader hold arriving? What must this page establish? What is it NOT responsible for?
2. Confirm: belief state leaving this page matches what the next page expects.
3. Review Common Failure Modes for this page type. Confirm none are present.
4. Verify mobile rendering. If it breaks on 375px, fix it.

## 1. PRESELL ADVERTORIAL

**Entry/exit:** Content-consumption mode --> B1-B4 established, clicks through to sales page.
**Conversion event:** Click-through (not purchase).
**Tone:** Editorial / journalistic. Must read like a wellness article. Third-person or personal-discovery narrative.
**Word count:** 800-1,200 words.

### Pre-Writing Gate: Promise Contract Verification

Before drafting, the writer must have the selected headline's **PROMISE_CONTRACT** (from HEADLINE-ENGINE.md Step 4.5). Verify that the DELIVERY_TEST can be satisfied within Sections 1-2 (230-320 combined words). If the headline promises specific informational content (physiological consequences, named mechanisms, specific data) that cannot fit within the editorial frame and word budget, select a different headline from the HookBank.

### Section Blueprint

| Pos | Section | Job | State Entering | State Leaving | Belief(s) | Words | Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Headline + Lead | Hook with specific herbal decision-making frustration in editorial frame. No product, no sales language. **If the headline's PROMISE_CONTRACT requires specific informational content, the Lead MUST begin delivering it here. The reader clicked for the promise — the lead is where payment begins.** | Scrolling feed, 2-sec window, zero context. | "This is about me." Reads on. | B1 + B2 seed | 80-120 | Avatar brief, VOC data, **Promise Contract** |
| 2 | Problem Expansion | Name contradictions in herbal info with concrete examples she recognizes. **PROMISE DELIVERY CHECKPOINT: If the headline's DELIVERY_TEST has not been substantially satisfied by the end of this section, the body has a structural problem. Sections 1-2 serve the headline promise. Sections 3-6 serve the belief chain. The promise must be delivered in the overlap zone.** | "This article gets it." | Frustration sharpened: "exactly my experience." | B2 | 150-200 | Competitor research, named herbs/confusion points |
| 3 | Ecosystem Indictment | Externalize blame to AI slop, affiliate blogs, anonymous advice -- the system is broken, not her. | Self-blaming ("maybe I'm not researching enough"). | Blame shifted externally. Relief + mild anger. | B3 (UMP) | 200-300 | UMP framework, ecosystem failure examples |
| 4 | Pivot to Solution Category | Bridge: "what you need is a decision system, not more reading." | Agrees ecosystem is broken, doesn't know the fix. | "I need a system." Solution category accepted. | B4 | 100-150 | B4 language, bridge framework (Subsection A) |
| 5 | System Tease | Introduce safety-first reference concept. First oblique product mention in final 25%. | Wants a system. Open to seeing one. | Curious about this specific system. | B4-B5 bridge | 100-150 | UMS pillars, product name |
| 6 | CTA | Single curiosity-driven CTA maintaining editorial frame. | "Show me." | Clicks through. | -- | 50-80 | CTA language (Subsection A) |

### Failure Modes

1. **Breaking editorial frame early.** Sales language, price mention, or benefit stack before final 25% = reader categorizes as ad and bounces.
2. **Vague indictment.** "Bad info out there" is not specific. Name: AI herb books on Amazon, contradictory Pinterest dosing charts, anonymous Facebook group advice.
3. **No externalization beat.** Without "it's not your fault," reader stays in self-blame and disengages.
4. **Overloading proof.** One embedded authority reference maximum. Save the proof stack for the sales page.
5. **Weak bridge.** "Learn more" is not a bridge. Promise something specific: "See the three safety checks the system runs before recommending any herb."
6. **Headline promise deferred past the pivot.** The headline opens an information loop (e.g., "what happens inside your body when..."). The body never closes it because Sections 3-6 are consumed by belief-chain progression (ecosystem indictment, pivot, system tease, CTA). Prevention: The headline's DELIVERY_TEST (from its Promise Contract) must be satisfied in Sections 1-2. If the template cannot accommodate the promised content, the headline is wrong for this page type — go back to the HookBank and select a different headline.

### CTA Rules

- One CTA, end of page. No mid-article CTAs (breaks editorial frame).
- Language: "See how it works" / "See the system" / "Here's what a safety-first reference looks like."
- Underlined text link is acceptable for maximum editorial feel.

## 2. LONG-FORM SALES PAGE

**Entry/exit:** Solution Aware (from presell) or Problem Aware (direct) --> B5-B8 established, clicks to checkout.
**Conversion event:** Purchase ($49).
**Tone:** Direct, authoritative, empathetic. Overtly persuasive, brand-voice governed.
**Word count:** 2,500-4,000 words.

### Section Blueprint

| Pos | Section | Job | State Entering | State Leaving | Belief(s) | Words | Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hero | Product name + primary benefit in desired-outcome framing. Reader knows what this is and who it's for. | Curious (presell) or exploratory (direct). | "This is the system" or "might be for me." | B5 seed | 30-50 | Product name, benefit, audience ID |
| 2 | Problem Recap | Compress B1-B4 for direct traffic; confirm "right place" for presell readers. | Presell: B1-B4 held. Direct: Problem Aware. | All aligned at B4. | B1-B4 recap | 150-200 | UMP summary, condensed agitation |
| 3 | Mechanism: The Honest System | Walk three UMS pillars (safety flags, confidence levels, when-not-to-DIY) proving structural differentiation. | "Is this the right system?" | "Genuinely different from every herb book I've seen." | B5 (UMS) | 400-600 | UMS framework, sample entry, competitive comparisons |
| 4 | Identity Bridge | Position "natural-first but not reckless" identity as compatible with using this system. | Intellectually convinced. Wondering "is this me?" | "This IS my approach." | B6 | 150-200 | Avatar identity language, VOC quotes |
| 5 | Social Proof | 3-5 scenario-specific testimonials. At least one references a safety decision. | Wants real-people evidence. | Skepticism reduced. | B5-B6 reinforcement | 300-400 | Testimonials, user stories |
| 6 | What's Inside | Feature-to-benefit walkthrough. Each feature tied to a UMS pillar. | "What exactly do I get?" | Clear deliverable picture. | B5 reinforcement | 200-300 | TOC, bonus list, format details |
| 7 | Bonus Stack | Bonuses as force multipliers, not value-inflation filler. | Evaluating total value. | "More than expected." | B7 seed | 150-200 | 4 bonuses with one-sentence benefits |
| 8 | Value Stack + Price | Anchor against naturopath visit, research hours, cost of one mistake. State $49. | Value forming. Price unknown. | "$49 is cheap for this." | B7 | 150-200 | Real price anchors |
| 9 | Guarantee | 30-day guarantee framed around quick validation: "Look up one herb. Check safety flags. If it fails your test, money back." | Last barrier: "what if it's not as good?" | "No risk. I can verify instantly." | B8 | 100-150 | Guarantee terms, validation prompt |
| 10 | Primary CTA | Purchase button with price. No ambiguity. | Ready to buy. | Clicks to checkout. | B7+B8 | 50-80 | Button copy, price |
| 11 | FAQ / Objection Sweep | Top 3-5 objections in Q&A. Biggest barrier first. Each answer closes, not just informs. | Didn't click CTA. Objections remain. | Objections neutralized. | B5-B8 | 200-300 | Objection list (offer brief) |
| 12 | Second CTA + P.S. | Guarantee restatement or single strongest proof point. Final prompt. | Objections handled. Needs last nudge. | Clicks or exits. | B8 | 80-120 | Best testimonial or guarantee |

### Failure Modes

1. **Mechanism reads as feature list.** Must explain WHY each pillar matters (drug interactions are the #1 unreported herbal risk), not just what they are.
2. **Identity Bridge skipped.** Reader agrees system works but feels "this is for health pros, not me." Bridge must use her language.
3. **Generic testimonials.** "Great book!" is worthless. Each must name a specific herb, situation, or decision.
4. **Value stack = math tricks.** "$10,000 value" is unbelievable. "$49 < one naturopath visit" is real.
5. **FAQ as afterthought.** Each answer is a mini-close. "Can I use this on meds?" answers AND references the interaction feature.
6. **Hero headline promise not addressed until Section 3+.** The Hero makes a specific promise (e.g., "the system that eliminates herbal guesswork"). If the Promise Contract's DELIVERY_TEST requires content that doesn't appear until the Mechanism section (Section 3), direct-traffic readers may bounce before reaching it. Prevention: the Problem Recap (Section 2) must begin paying off the hero's promise, not just recap the problem. If the headline's DELIVERY_TEST can't be served by Section 2, reconsider the headline.

### CTA Rules

- **Primary (Sec 10):** "Get the Handbook -- $49." Price on button. Full-width mobile.
- **Secondary (Sec 12):** Same copy. Preceded by P.S.
- **Sticky bar:** Appears after Section 6. "Get the Handbook" (no price on sticky). Does not obscure content.
- **Max 3 CTA placements.** More than three feels desperate for this audience.

## 3. CHECKOUT PAGE

**Entry/exit:** High purchase intent --> purchase complete, zero confusion about delivery.
**Conversion event:** Complete purchase.
**Tone:** Reassuring, minimal. Every word reduces friction or reinforces the decision.
**Word count:** 200-400 words (excludes form fields).

### Section Blueprint

| Pos | Section | Job | State Entering | State Leaving | Belief(s) | Words | Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Order Summary | Restate deliverable in benefit language, not line-item format. | "Am I making the right call?" | "Yes, clearly stated." | B7 reinforcement | 60-80 | Product + format + bonuses |
| 2 | Trust Signals | Security badges, guarantee reminder, support contact -- clustered near payment fields. | Anxiety spikes at CC form. | "Secure. Guaranteed. Supported." | B8 reinforcement | 40-60 | SSL, payment logos, guarantee badge, support email |
| 3 | Order Bump | Cheat Sheets ($9-$19) as natural enhancement. One sentence, one checkbox, one price. | Low cognitive bandwidth. | Adds or ignores. No friction. | -- | 60-100 | Bump product, price, one benefit |
| 4 | Submit + Microcopy | Confirm decision. One reassurance line below button. | Final doubt. | Clicks submit. | B8 | 30-50 | Button: "Complete My Order & Get Instant Access." Sub-button: "30-day guarantee. Instant delivery. Yours forever." |

### Failure Modes

1. **Generic line item.** "1x Handbook -- $49" wastes space. Say: "Handbook (PDF + printable sheets + 4 bonuses) -- $49."
2. **Trust signals below fold.** Must be visible when payment form is visible.
3. **Bump copy too long.** If it needs a paragraph, it's too complex for this placement.
4. **Button says "Pay Now."** Loss framing. Use "Complete My Order."
5. **No delivery indication.** Reader must know: instant PDF, email confirmation, start immediately.

### Mobile Additions

- Single-column only. Numeric keyboard for card fields, email keyboard for email.
- Bump checkbox: 44px minimum. Accidental adds destroy trust.
- Submit button: fixed at viewport bottom when form visible. 54px height minimum.
- No reload during processing. Inline "Processing..." state.

## 4. UPSELL PAGE

**Entry/exit:** Post-purchase high --> accepts upsell or proceeds to downsell.
**Conversion event:** Add upsell to order.
**Tone:** Congratulatory, then consultative. NOT a second sales page.
**Word count:** 400-800 words.
**Product:** Home Apothecary Starter Kit video walkthrough ($79-$149).

### Section Blueprint

| Pos | Section | Job | State Entering | State Leaving | Belief(s) | Words | Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Confirmation | Affirm purchase before anything else. | Post-purchase disorientation. | "Went through. Good decision." | -- | 40-60 | Product name, confirmation, delivery timeline |
| 2 | Bridge | Connect upsell to handbook: "You have the reference. Here's what makes it work in your kitchen." | Validated. Not expecting offer. | "Natural next step." Curiosity. | -- | 60-100 | Bridge: handbook = knowledge, kit = application |
| 3 | Upsell Offer | Single clear benefit: "See the top 10 remedies prepared step by step on video." | Curious. Evaluating. | "This would help me use the handbook." | B5 extension | 150-250 | Product details, format, top 3 benefits |
| 4 | Quick Proof | One testimonial or specific example in context of handbook. | Interested, not convinced. | Social validation. | B5-B6 | 60-100 | One scenario testimonial |
| 5 | Price + CTA | Price, value anchor, clear yes/no. | Ready to decide. | Clicks yes or no. | B7 extension | 80-120 | Price, value anchor, button copy |

### Critical Rule: Never Undermine the Core Purchase

**Prohibited:** "The handbook alone isn't enough..." / "Without the videos, you might struggle..." / "Most people also need..."
**Approved:** "The handbook gives you knowledge. The kit shows hands-on technique." / "You'll use the handbook day one. Videos make first preparations faster."

### Failure Modes

1. **No purchase confirmation first.** "Wait! Don't close this page!" triggers instant buyer's remorse.
2. **Feels like a separate sale.** Must feel like extension of same decision, not new decision.
3. **Too long.** 60-90 second read window. If longer, cut.
4. **Guilt-based decline.** "No, I don't care about health" is manipulative. Use: "No thanks, just the handbook for now."
5. **Price buried or hidden.** State it plainly. This audience punishes deception.

### CTA Rules

- Yes: "Yes -- Add the Starter Kit to My Order [$price]."
- No: "No thanks, just the handbook for now." Same font size as body. Never miniaturized or hidden.
- One yes button, one no link.

## 5. DOWNSELL PAGE

**Entry/exit:** Declined upsell, friction fatigue --> accepts downsell or exits to email onboarding.
**Conversion event:** Add downsell to order.
**Tone:** Generous, understanding, brief. Thoughtful alternative, not desperate grab.
**Word count:** 200-400 words.
**Product:** 3 essential remedy videos only ($29-$39), or payment plan for full kit.

### Section Blueprint

| Pos | Section | Job | State Entering | State Leaving | Belief(s) | Words | Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Acknowledgment | Validate the no: "Totally understand -- the full kit isn't for everyone right now." | Guard up. Expecting pressure. | "Not pressuring me." Guard lowers. | -- | 20-40 | Upsell product name |
| 2 | Reduced Offer | Smaller, cheaper alternative: "But I didn't want you to miss the 3 essential videos -- the ones most people use in their first week." | Slightly open. | "More manageable. Relevant." | B7 extension | 100-180 | Downsell specifics, price, contrast to upsell |
| 3 | CTA + Decline | Clear yes/no. Same respectful framing. | Quick decision. | Accepts or declines without resentment. | B8 | 50-80 | Button copy, price, no copy |

### Failure Modes

1. **"WAIT!" energy.** Urgency screams = brand-toxic. Calm, generous only.
2. **Same product, lower price.** Trains discount-waiting. Must be genuinely different/smaller, or a payment plan.
3. **Over 400 words.** Patience is near zero. Get in, offer, get out.
4. **Guilt for declining.** Any "you'll regret this" implication violates brand voice.

### CTA Rules

- Yes: "Yes, Add the 3 Essential Videos -- $29." Price on button.
- No: "No thanks, I'm all set."
- One yes, one no. Nothing else.

## 6. EMAIL SEQUENCE TEMPLATES

### Universal Email Rules

**Subject lines:** 4-9 words. Lowercase-friendly. No ALL CAPS. Pass the "would I open this from a friend?" test. First-name personalization max 1 in 5 emails.
**Structure:** Opening line = preview text (40-60 chars). One job per email. One CTA per email. CTA as bold text link on its own line. Signature consistent: "-- [Name], The Honest Herbalist."
**Mobile:** Design for 100% mobile. Max 600px width. No multi-column. Links on own line, tappable. No images carrying essential meaning.

---

### 6A. Welcome / Confirmation Email

**Trigger:** Immediately post-purchase. **Job:** Deliver access, affirm decision. **Words:** 150-250.

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Subject | "Your Honest Herbalist Handbook is ready" -- delivery-framed, no theatrics. |
| Opening | Delivery link first. No fluff before it. |
| Body | 1. Download link. 2. Quick-start: "Look up one herb you use. Check the safety flags." 3. "Tomorrow you'll get tips for getting the most out of it." |
| CTA | "Download Your Handbook." |
| Failure mode | Burying download link below congratulatory copy. She wants her product -- deliver it. |

### 6B. Nurture Email (Belief-Building)

**Trigger:** Day 2-5, pre-sale sequence. **Job:** Advance one belief per email via story/education. **Words:** 200-350.

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Subject | Curiosity/identity hook: "the elderberry dosing debate" / "she thought chamomile was always safe." |
| Opening | Story or question. Never "Hi [name], hope you're well." |
| Body | One belief per email. Agitate-Empathy-Bridge (Subsection A). E1 = B2, E2 = B3, E3 = B4. |
| CTA | Soft: "See what a safety-first system looks like." Link to sales page. |
| Failure mode | Building two beliefs in one email. Starting with "I." CTA disconnected from story. |

### 6C. Pre-Sale Email (Urgency / Value)

**Trigger:** Day 5-7 or tied to real deadline. **Job:** Convert nurtured non-buyers. **Words:** 150-300.

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Subject | Direct: "the handbook closes Friday" / "$49 for the whole system." |
| Opening | One-sentence value prop. No preamble. |
| Body | Value recap (2-3 bullets). Urgency (ONLY if real). Guarantee reminder. |
| CTA | "Get the Handbook Before [Deadline]." |
| Failure mode | Fake urgency (detected instantly). Entire sales page in email. No guarantee mention. |

### 6D. Cart Abandonment Email

**Trigger:** 1-4 hours post-abandonment. **Job:** Address reason, provide clean path back. **Words:** 100-200.

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Subject | Helpful: "your order wasn't completed -- need help?" |
| Opening | "You started an order but didn't finish." No pressure. |
| Body | 1. Ask about technical issues. 2. Restate guarantee. 3. Direct link to pre-filled checkout. |
| CTA | "Complete Your Order" -- to checkout, not sales page. |
| Failure mode | Aggressive subject ("You forgot!"). More than 2 abandonment emails. Offering a discount (trains behavior). |

### 6E. Post-Purchase Onboarding Email

**Trigger:** 24-48 hours post-purchase. **Job:** Drive first meaningful use. **Words:** 200-300.

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Subject | "try this 2-minute handbook test" / "your first lookup: start here." |
| Opening | Direct to action: "Look up an herb you already have in your kitchen." |
| Body | 1. Specific first action (chamomile safety flags). 2. What to look for (2-3 sentences). 3. Reply invitation: "Tell me which herb you looked up first." |
| CTA | "Open Your Handbook." No selling. |
| Failure mode | Upsell in onboarding (burns trust). Vague instructions. Skipping reply invite (kills engagement + deliverability). |

## 7. ORDER CONFIRMATION / THANK YOU PAGE

**Entry/exit:** Purchase complete --> buyer's remorse neutralized, product accessed, upsell path set.
**Conversion event:** None (secondary: upsell bridge click or first product use).
**Tone:** Warm, affirming, practical.
**Word count:** 150-300 words.

### Section Blueprint

| Pos | Section | Job | State Entering | State Leaving | Belief(s) | Words | Inputs |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Confirmation | Confirm order, affirm decision. Make her feel smart, not lucky. | "Did it go through?" | "Confirmed. Good decision." | B7 reinforcement | 40-60 | Product name, order number |
| 2 | Delivery | Exactly how to get handbook, what format, when. Zero ambiguity. | "Where's my thing?" | "I know what to do." | -- | 50-80 | Download link, format, email note |
| 3 | Quick-Start | One specific small action: "Look up chamomile. Check safety flags." | "Now what?" | "I know where to start." | B5 reinforcement | 40-80 | First-action suggestion |
| 4 | Upsell Bridge | Transition to upsell as natural next step, clearly separated from confirmation. | Satisfied, open. | Clicks through with curiosity. | -- | 30-60 | Bridge: "One more thing that pairs with your handbook..." |

### Failure Modes

1. **No confirmation.** Uncertainty = anxiety spike. First line: "Your order is confirmed."
2. **Download buried.** Must be visible without scrolling.
3. **No quick-start.** Reader who doesn't open within 24 hours = higher refund rate.
4. **Upsell bridge = bait-and-switch.** Bridge must be brief, optional, below the fold, clearly separated.

### CTA Rules

- Primary: "Download Your Handbook" / "Access Your Handbook Now." Delivery CTA, not purchase CTA.
- Secondary (bridge): "See what pairs with your handbook." Soft, curiosity-forward.
- No social shares, surveys, or "tell a friend." Focus.
